ABOUT SPEED TYPES
CAS — Calibrated Airspeed
Airspeed measured by the pitot tube and corrected for instrument error. At low altitude and speed: IAS ≈ CAS ≈ EAS.
TAS — True Airspeed
Actual speed relative to the air mass. As altitude increases, air density decreases and TAS > CAS.
EAS — Equivalent Airspeed
Speed that produces the same dynamic pressure as TAS at sea level. EAS = TAS × √(ρ/ρ₀)
MACH — Mach Number
Ratio of TAS to the local speed of sound. M < 0.8 subsonic, 0.8–1.2 transonic, > 1.2 supersonic.
